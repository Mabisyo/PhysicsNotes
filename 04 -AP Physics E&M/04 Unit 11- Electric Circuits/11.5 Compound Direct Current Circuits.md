# Concepts

## Equivalent Resistance for Series and Parallel
### Resistors in Series
- Current only has one path to travel 
- Current is the same for all resistors on the path
- More obstacles on the path will result in more resistance
### Resistors in Parallel
- Current has multiple paths it could travel 
- Potential difference is the same across all resistors in the combo
- More paths result in less equivalent resistance
## Ideal vs non-Ideal Batteries & Wires
### Batteries
- Ideal batteries have negligible internal resistance
- The voltage a battery would supply if it were ideal is the same as the voltage across the terminals of a non-ideal battery with no current flowing
	- Sometimes called “Electromotive force”
- Internal resistance can be treated as a regular resistor
- Terminal voltage **DECREASES** as internal resistance **INCREASES**
### Wires
- Ideal wires have negligible resistance
- If a wire is a good conductor, is resistance is much smaller than the load so it usually neglected
	- It can *only* be neglected if there is a load
### Ideal Battery
*Terminal Voltage is the same as electromotive force (EMF)*
### Non-Ideal Battery
*The battery uses some voltage on itself (Lesser Voltage), resulting in a greater EMF*

## Ammeters & Voltmeters

***Non-Ideal Ammeters and voltmeters will change the properties of the circuit being measured***
### Ammeter
*A tool used to measure current at a specific point in a circuit*
- Ammeters must be connected in series with the element in which current is being measured
- Ideal ammeters have zero resistance so that they do not affect the current in the element that they are in series with
### Voltmeter
*A tool used to measure electric potential difference (voltage) between two points in a circuit*
- Voltmeters must be connected in parallel with the element across which voltage is being measured
- Ideal voltmeters have infinite resistance so that no charge flows through them
