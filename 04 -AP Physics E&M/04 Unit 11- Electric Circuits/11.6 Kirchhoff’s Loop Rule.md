# Kirchhoff’s Loop Rule
- The sum of all voltages in a closed loop equals zero, given by…
$$\sum \Delta V_{loop}=0$$
- The direction and signs of each individual voltage representation is extremely important in this formula. This means that…
	- Current going through a battery (negative to positive terminal), then your voltage is positive.
	- Current going against a battery (positive to negative terminal), then your voltage is negative.
	- Current going through a resistor is negative, unless traveling against the direction of current, which makes it positive.
- You apply Kirchhoff’s Loop Rule to every single individual loop, not the entire circuit itself. Meaning if you have a parallel circuit that has components in series, you apply Kirchhoff’s Loop Rule per circuit. An individual loop is one that contains at least one branch not in any other loop.
- After setting up the loop rule for each individual loop, you can solve the system of equations to find the magnitude of the current for each of them.
	- If you get a negative current, all it means is that the current moves in the opposite direction that you assigned it to move. The math works itself out.
