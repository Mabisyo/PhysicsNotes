## Electric Current
*Electric Current* (I): 
	The amount of charge (Q) moving through a cross sectional area of a wire in time interval **t**.
- Current is measured in Amps or Amperes (A), which is the number of coulombs of charge per second (C/s).
*Drift Velocity*:
	The net charged particle motion of all the particles within an area

*Electric Current Depends on the following*:
- The number of charged particles per cubic meter of volume
- Quantity of Charge
- Cross sectional area of charge

*Considerations:*
- In order to have current in a wire, there must be an electric potential difference, referred to as electromotive force.
- The individual speed of each charged particle is always greater than the drift speed.

## Charge Flow
- If there is an electric field in a wire, then the charges in that wire will be forced to move.
- If the strength of the electric field is increased, the force is increased, which increases the electric current density and are vectors. 
- Electric current is based on the charges that are moving in a conductor, which does have a direction.
- Electrons in a wire move in the opposite direction of conventional current.
