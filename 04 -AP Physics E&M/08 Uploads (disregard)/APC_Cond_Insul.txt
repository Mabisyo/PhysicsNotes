Conductors v Insulators:

1. Conductors

-Charges move freely inside the material.

-Electric field inside = 0 at electrostatic equilibrium.

-Excess charge resides on the surface.

-Equipotential: entire conductor same potential.
-Examples: metals (Cu, Al, Au).

2. Insulators

-Charges do not move freely.

-Electric field can exist inside.

-Charge stays where placed.

-No natural equipotential.

-Examples: rubber, glass, plastic.
