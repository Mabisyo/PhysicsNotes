Unit 8: Electric Charges, Fields, and Gauss Law:

Work done by field = -U

Positive particles moving along the direction of the electric field => positive work done by the field.

R = radius of the sphere
Q = total charge of the sphere
r = distance from the center
E - electric field
K= couloumb's constant
Q = total charge of sphere, rod, system..
lamba = linear charge density 
s.c.d = surface charge density

1. Electric field from a uniformly charged sphere (inside & outside):

E(r) = (KQ)/(r^2) r>=R (outside)

E(r) = (KQr)/(R^3) (inside) 

Steps: 
Pick a Gaussian surface: sphere of radius r centered on the charge.

Check r: if r < R it's inside, if r >= R it's outside.

Find enclosed charge:

Outside: all charge Q.

Inside: fraction proportional to volume it's Q enclosed = Q * (r^3 / R^3).

Apply Gauss Law:
E * surface_area = Q_enclosed/eps0
Surface_area = 4 * pi * r^2

Solve for E.
Direction: radial, away from center (for positive charge).


2. Electric field of infinite line of charge

E = (lamba)/(eps0 * 2pi * r) 

Pick a Gaussian surface: cylinder of radius r around the wire.

Use symmetry: field points radially outward.

Flux through cylinder:
flux = E * (2 * pi * r * length)
Q_enclosed = lambda * length
flux = Q_enclosed / e0

And! Solve


3. Electric field of an infinite plane

E = (s.c.d)/(2eps0)

Pick a Gaussian surface a pillbox that straddles the sheet.

Check symmetry: field perpendicular to sheet, same on both sides.

Flux through pillbox:

flux = (E * top_area) + (E * bottom_area) = 2 * E * A

Q_enclosed = sigma * A

flux = Q_enclosed / e0 → E = sigma / (2 * e0)





4. Electric field of a ring of charge (along the axis)

R = radius of the ring
Q = total charge of the ring
x = distance from the center along the axis of the ring

E = (KQx)/((x^2 + R^2)^(3/2))

Steps (derivation likely not needed):


Break the ring into small charge elements dq.

Each dq produces a small electric field dE.

Because of symmetry, horizontal components cancel.

Only the components along the axis add together.

Distance from dq to the point:

r = sqrt(x^2 + R^2)

Electric field from dq:

dE = (K dq)/(r^2)

Component along the axis:

dE_axis = dE * cos(theta)

From geometry:

cos(theta) = x/r

Substitute:

dE_axis = (K dq)/(r^2) * (x/r)

dE_axis = (Kx dq)/(r^3)

Since r = sqrt(x^2 + R^2), r^3 = (x^2 + R^2)^(3/2)

dE_axis = (Kx dq)/((x^2 + R^2)^(3/2))

Integrate around the ring:

∫dq = Q

Final result:

E = (KQx)/((x^2 + R^2)^(3/2))

Direction: along the axis of the ring.
