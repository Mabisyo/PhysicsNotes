# Capacitors
_________
* Capacitors are made up of two conductors, separated by some distance, that carry equal but opposite charges +Q and -Q.
* Work must be done to create this separation of charge, storing potential energy.
* Capacitors are basic storage devices for electrical potential energy.
* The conductors used may have any shape, but the most common capacitors are parallel metal plates or sheets, called **parallel-plate capacitors**. 
* The electric field due to *one* plate with a surface charge density of: $$\sigma = \frac{Q}{A}$$ is given by the equation: $$E = \frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_{0}}$$ with E pointing away from the plate when sigma is positive, and towards the plate when sigma is negative.
* With two plates, one with surface charge density +sigma and the other with -sigma, the fields combine to give a field that equals 0 outside the plates and has a magnitude of: $$E_{total}=\frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_{0}}+\frac{\sigma}{2\varepsilon_{0}}=\frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_{0}}$$ substituting the potential difference between the two plates for the E field, we get: $$E=\frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_{0}} \space \to \frac{\Delta V}{d}=\frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon_{0}} \to \frac{\Delta V}{d}= \frac{\frac{Q}{A}}{\varepsilon_{0}} \to \frac{Q}{\Delta V}=\frac{\varepsilon_{0}A}{d}$$
* The ratio of Q to V for *any* capacitor is called its **capacitance** (C) $$\boxed{C=\frac{Q}{\Delta V}}$$ so for a parallel-plate capacitor we get: $$C=\frac{\varepsilon_{0}A}{d}$$
* Capacitance measure the capacity for holding charge, the greater the capacitance, the more charge can be stored on the plates at a given potential difference. (Depends on the size, shape, and distance of separation).
	* The units are coulombs per volt, or **farads**. 1 C/V = 1 F
