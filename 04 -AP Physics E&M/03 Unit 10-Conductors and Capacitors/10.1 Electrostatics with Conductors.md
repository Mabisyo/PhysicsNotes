# Conductors and Insulators
* Material that permit the flow of charge are called **conductors**; they conduct electricity. Metals are the best examples of this, but other conductors are aqueous solutions that contain dissolved electrolytes (Eg. Salt water).
* In the presence of an electric field, the electrons are free to move about the lattice of conductor materials, creating of “sea” of mobile electrons, allowing excess charge to flow freely.
* The outmost electrons in an **insulator** have no such mobility; There is no pathway because the required energy to mobilize electrons is large.
* Between conductors and insulators is a class of materials known as **semiconductors**. They are less conducting than most metals, but more conducting than most insulators (Eg. Silicon and Germanium).
* Extreme examples of conductors are **superconductors**. This is a *perfect* conductors and allows absolutely no resistance to the flow of charge. (Many metals and ceramics at extremely low temps.)
