# Electric Charge
**What is Electric Charge?**
**Definition**: A fundamental property of matter that causes it to experience electromagnetic forces. It is not the same as Energy, Force, Motion, or Magnetism, it simply describes how particles interact electrically.
**Types of Charges**
- Positive (+): Protons
- Negative (-): Electrons
- Neutral: Neutrons
The Proton and Electron have equal magnitude and opposite sign charge.
**Conservation of Charge**: Net charge in an isolated system remains constant
**Quantization of Charge**: Charge exists in discrete packets (8.2) of electron charge, called an elementary charge…
$$ e = 1.602(10)^{-19}C$$
The unit is in Coulombs, which is 1 Amp * 1 Second.

**What does Electric Charge Do?**
Electric charges create electric force, which can either repel or attract. Like charges repel, and opposite charges attract.


# Coulomb’s Law (Electric Force between Point Charges)

**Equation**:
$$
\vec{F}_e = k_e\frac{|q_1q_2|}{r^2}\widehat{r}
$$
- k sub e is Coulomb’s Constant (provided on Equation sheet)
- The “q” represent charge (C) of 1 particle
- r represents the distance between charges (m)
- r with wide-hat represents the unit vector in the direction of the force

This equation defines the electric force between 2 particles of q charge at r distance. Electric force is proportional to electric charge, decreases with distance squared (inverse square law), and acts along line r. In order to determine where the force points, recall that opposite charges point towards each other, while same charges repel each other. This equation calculates the magnitude of the force, and the r hat is defined by you determining if the force points in between charges, or away from each other.

# Superposition Principle
The idea that multiple charges do not interfere with each other, they simply add together. Since Electric forces are linear, they add together. Each force is calculated as if the others did not exist, then you add vectors. This is because electric forces obey linear differential equations (more on that in Gauss’s Law). So if they pull opposite, they cancel, if they pull at angles, use components. This applies to Forces, Electric Fields, Electric Potential, and Magnetic Fields.


# Electric Field (E) due to Point Charges
In Coulombs law, to find the electric force, it is required that you have 2 charges to find the force of one of them (Remember Newton’s 3rd Law).  So, the electric field was developed, and the use of test charges is explored more in 8.3, Electric fields.
**Definition**: Force per unit charge experienced by a test charge (initial charge)
