## Flux
*Flux is an amount of stuff going through an area*
**Magnetic Flux quantifies how much magnetic field passes through a surface. It is the foundation of Faraday’s Law, which governs all of electromagnetic induction.** Magnetic Flux is analogous to electric flux.
- **Only the component of B perpendicular to the surface contributes to flux.**
- Flux is a **scalar** quantity (it has no direction), even though B and dA are vectors.
- A **changing** flux is what drives induced EMF (Faraday's Law, covered in 13.2).
## Core Equations

### Uniform Field — Simple Case

$$\Phi_B = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA\cos\theta$$

|Symbol|Meaning|Units|
|---|---|---|
|Φ_B|Magnetic flux|Weber (Wb) = T·m²|
|B|Magnetic field magnitude|Tesla (T)|
|A|Area of surface|m²|
|θ|Angle between **B** and the **area normal vector**|degrees / radians|
**θ is measured from the normal (area vector), NOT from the plane of the surface.**
### Non-Uniform Field — Calculus Form (AP Exam Required)

$$\Phi_B = \int_A \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A}$$

- Used when **B varies** over the surface, or the surface is **curved**
- Break the surface into infinitesimal area patches dA, compute the dot product at each point, then integrate
- dA = differential area **vector**: magnitude = area element, direction = normal to the surface
### Gauss's Law for Magnetism

$$\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$$

- The **net** magnetic flux through any **closed surface** is always **zero**
- Physical meaning: there are **no magnetic monopoles** — field lines always form closed loops
- Every field line that enters a closed surface must exit it
## The Area Vector (d**A**)

The area vector is crucial for computing flux correctly.

- **Direction:** Perpendicular (normal) to the surface
- For **open surfaces** (a loop): direction is chosen by the **right-hand rule**
    - Curl the fingers of your right hand in the direction of current (or chosen loop direction)
    - Thumb points in the direction of the area vector
- For **closed surfaces**: area vector points **outward by convention**
## Special Cases

|Scenario|θ|cos θ|Flux|
|---|---|---|---|
|B ⊥ surface (B ∥ area vector)|0°|1|Φ_B = BA **(maximum)**|
|B at 45° to surface normal|45°|√2/2|Φ_B = BA(√2/2)|
|B ∥ surface (B ⊥ area vector)|90°|0|Φ_B = 0 **(zero)**|
|B anti-parallel to area vector|180°|-1|Φ_B = -BA **(negative)**|

## Sign of Magnetic Flux

Flux **can be positive or negative** — the sign depends on the dot product:

- **Positive** → B has a component **in the same direction** as the area vector
- **Negative** → B has a component **opposite** to the area vector
- Sign convention matters for Faraday's Law (change in sign = change in direction of induced EMF)

## 🎯 AP Exam Tips

> [!tip] Exam Strategy
> 
> - Always **define your area vector direction** before computing flux — state it explicitly in FRQ answers
> - When B is non-uniform, **set up the integral** even if you can't solve it — partial credit awarded
> - Distinguish between the angle from the **normal** (use in Φ = BA cosθ) vs. angle from the **plane** (complement: use sin instead)
> - Gauss's Law for magnetism (∮ B·dA = 0) is one of **Maxwell's Four Equations** — know this!
> - Flux is a **scalar** — don't try to give it a direction

> [!warning] Common Mistakes
> 
> - Confusing the angle: if given the angle between B and the **plane** of the loop, use **sin θ** not cos θ
> - Forgetting units: always express flux in **Webers (Wb)** = T·m²
> - Using the loop area instead of the solenoid area (or vice versa) in nested geometry problems

## 📝 Key Vocabulary

| Term                    | Definition                                                                     |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Magnetic Flux (Φ_B)** | Measure of magnetic field "threading" through a surface                        |
| **Weber (Wb)**          | SI unit of magnetic flux; 1 Wb = 1 T·m²                                        |
| **Area Vector (dA)**    | Vector normal to a surface; magnitude = area element                           |
| **Dot Product**         | Mathematical operation giving the scalar projection of one vector onto another |
| **Magnetic Monopole**   | Hypothetical isolated N or S pole — does not exist; proven by ∮B·dA = 0        |
| **Right-Hand Rule**     | Convention to determine area vector direction for an open surface              |
