# Concepts
_________________
An LR circuit contains at minimum a battery, a resistor, and an inductor in series. When the switch is closed or opened, the current does not respond instantly – the inductor’s back-EMF forces a smooth, exponential transition. The inductors maintains whatever current it currently has. It resists increases (by opposing the battery) and resists decreases (by trying to keep current flowing). The resistor is what ultimately limits and dissipates the energy. 

#### Current Growth (Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law)
$$\begin{array}
\epsilon - V_{R}-V_{L}=0 \\
\epsilon - IR - L \frac{dI}{dt} = 0 \\ \\
\frac{dI}{dt}=\frac{\epsilon}{L}-\frac{R}{L}I= \frac{\epsilon-IR}{L} \\
\frac{dI}{\frac{\epsilon}{R}-I}=\frac{R}{L}dt \\
-\ln\left( \frac{\epsilon}{R} - I \right)=\frac{R}{L}t+C \\
I(t)=\frac{\epsilon}{R}(1-e^{-t/\tau}), \tau=\frac{L}{R}
\end{array}$$ 

| Tau Value | Current During growth | Current during decay |
| --------- | --------------------- | -------------------- |
| t=0       | 0% of I_Final         | 100% of I₀           |
| t=τ       | 67% of I_Final        | 36.8% of I₀          |
| t=2τ      | 86.5% of I_Final      | 13.5% of I₀          |
| t=5τ      | 99.3% of I_Final      | 0.7% of I₀           |


#### Current Decay
With battery removed, KVL gives:
$$\begin{array}
-IR-L \frac{dI}{dt} = 0 \rightarrow \frac{dI}{dt}=-\frac{R}{L}I \\
I(t)=I_{0}e^{-t/\tau}, \tau=\frac{L}{R} \\
\end{array}$$

*At time t=0, the current in series with the inductor is 0 as the inductor opposes the rapid change in current. After a long time, the inductor will no longer oppose the rapid change in current and will act like a wire.*

#### Energy Stored in an Inductor
Energy stored in inductor at steady state:
$$U_{L}= \frac{1}{2}L\left( \frac{\epsilon}{R} \right)^2$$
$$P_{R}=I^2R$$
Total energy dissipated during decay (integrating I^2R from 0 to ∞):
$$\int_{0}^∞I^2_{0}e^{-2t/\tau}Rdt=I^2_{0}R \frac{\tau}{2}= I^2_{0}R \frac{L}{2R}=\frac{1}{2}LI^2_{0}$$
## Vocabulary

| Term                    | Definition                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Time Constant τ         | Sets the timescale for exponential approach to steady state                                  |
| Transient               | The time-varying part of the solution; dies away after ~5τ                                   |
| Steady State            | Long-time behavior; dI/dt = 0; V_L = 0, I=∈/R                                                |
| Initial Condition       | Value of I at t=0; needed to solve the differentially (I(0)=0 when starting from no current) |
| Thevenin Resistance     | Equivalent resistance seen by the inductor; used to compute τ                                |
| Inductive Time Constant | Another name for τ=L/R in an LR circuit                                                      |
