## Inductors 
*An inductor is a circuit element that opposes changes in current* by generating a back EMF(∈). Energy stored in an inductor lives in its magnetic field, exactly as energy stored in a capacitor lives in its electric field. 
#### Solenoid
*Solenoids consist of a wire tightly wrapped around a cylindrical core to create a helical stack of conducting loop. When current is established in the solenoid, a magnetic field exists through the core.* 
- **Magnetic Field Strength inside a Solenoid**: $$B_{sol}=\mu_{0}\frac{N}{l}I$$
If current changes, the flux through the coil changes, and by Faraday’s Law an EMF is induced in the coil itself. This is called self inductance. The induced EMF always opposes the change in current (Lenz’s Law), making the inductor “sluggish,” it resists both increases and decreases in current. 

#### Self Inductance
$$ \epsilon_{L} = -L\frac{dI}{dt}$$
- With a link to flux: $$L = \frac{N \phi_{B}}{I}$$
